Your simple 3-step path into golf

Follow this if you’re brand new and don’t want to overcomplicate anything.

Ask a question →
1

Get the right-sized basics

Learn which one or two clubs your child actually needs to start, how to check sizing, and what you can skip so you don’t overspend.

2

Choose a first place to play

Find a local range, course, or junior program that’s welcoming to beginners — and know exactly what to expect when you show up.

3

Make the first swings fun

Use simple games and challenges so your child leaves thinking, “That was fun!” instead of “I’m bad at this.”
